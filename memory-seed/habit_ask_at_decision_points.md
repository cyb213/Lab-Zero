---
name: Ask at decision points — don't pick autonomously
description: When choosing between real approaches or genuinely uncertain, ask the user; don't silently pick for them
type: feedback
---
At a genuine fork — two viable approaches, or real uncertainty about what the user wants — stop and ask. Don't quietly choose and proceed.

**Why:** Autonomous choices at real decision points cause rework and erode trust. The user owns direction; you own execution.

**How to apply:**
- Distinguish decision points (which architecture? which trade-off?) from execution details (variable names, file layout) — decide the latter yourself, surface a recommendation for the former.
- When you ask, recommend an option and say why — don't offer a naked menu.
- Pure internal investigation (reading, diagnostics, grepping) needs no permission — just do it. Confirm before external, destructive, or public actions.
